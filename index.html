Who We Are

Economist-Byline Africa

Discover our non-partisan, social enterprise platform dedicated to driving economic growth and development in Africa.

We gather a vibrant community of interdisciplinary scholars, policymakers, and development practitioners operating on the continent, providing educational resources, policy engagement, and fostering collaboration.

What we offer:

Educational Audio-Visual Content:

Access engaging and informative audio-visual content that covers a wide range of topics relevant to Africa's economic growth and development. Our content aims to promote learning, awareness, and critical thinking among our audience.

Expert Literature Review and Analysis:

Benefit from our team of experts who conduct in-depth reviews and analyses of scientific articles and publications. By synthesizing and distilling knowledge, we contribute to the existing body of research and provide valuable insights for policymakers.

Capacity Building Programs:

Enhance your skills and understanding of economic development through our capacity-building programs. Designed for young economists, policymakers, and development practitioners, our programs equip participants with the tools necessary to drive positive change in Africa.

Thematic Areas:

The following are the key seven (7) areas of focus of Economist-Byline Africa:

No Poverty

Africa has a significant proportion of its population living in poverty. Economic policies and programmes that focus on poverty eradication, social protection, and inclusive growth are crucial in achieving this goal. These policies and programmes may include targeted interventions for vulnerable populations, access to quality education and healthcare, and promoting decent employment opportunities.

Decent Work and Economic Growth:

This goal emphasizes the need for sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all. Economic policies and programmes in Africa that focus on creating job opportunities, promoting entrepreneurship, and ensuring fair wages and safe working conditions.

Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure:

Building resilient infrastructure, promoting inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and fostering innovation are essential for economic development in Africa. Investment in infrastructure projects, support for technological advancements, and policies and programmes that attract foreign direct investment can all contribute to achieving this goal.
